# M&A Signal Intelligence Engine — Financial Glossary

## Deal Participants
- **Buyer**: party acquiring the target company or asset
- **Seller**: party selling the target company or asset
- **Target**: company or asset being acquired
- **Family Office**: private entity that manages the wealth and investments of one or more ultra-high-net-worth families
- **Private Equity**: investment firm that raises capital to acquire, build, and exit companies for a return

## Deal Types
- **Acquisition**: purchase of a controlling stake in a company or asset
- **Divestment**: sale of a business, business unit, or asset
- **Merger**: combination of two companies into one entity
- **Minority Stake**: purchase of less than 50% of a company
- **Asset Rotation**: sale of assets to redeploy capital into new ones
- **Distressed Sale**: sale driven by financial pressure, refinancing risk, covenant breach, or liquidity stress

## Process Terms
- **Competitive Tension**: pressure created by multiple active buyers competing for the same asset

## Financial Metrics - Companies
- **EV (Enterprise Value)**: the primary reference point for deal valuations. the total value of a business to all capital providers, typically equity value plus net debt and other adjustments
- **Net Debt**: gross debt excluding cash and cash equivalents
- **Equity Value (EqV)**: value attributable to shareholders after debt and cash adjustments
- **EBITDA**: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortisation. Proxy for operating cash flow

## Valuation Metrics
- **EV/EBITDA**: most common M&A valuation multiple for companies. Higher = more expensive
- **EV/MW**: EV per megawatt of installed capacity. Primary valuation metric for renewable energy portfolios
- **Cap rate**: net operating income divided by property value, used as a real estate valuation yield

## Signal Intelligence Terms
- **M&A Signal**: event or data point indicating possible buying or selling activity
- **Strategic Driver**: the underlying reason behind a transaction
- **Urgency**: how quickly a signal requires action from an M&A team
- **Match Score**: 0.0-1.0 fit between a buyer and an acquisition target
